GameDev Class Final Project

The Last Postman 2076

This was the final for a narrative games design class, where the assignment was to use one of several small game engines to create our final product. I ended up choosing to learn how to use and develop the whole game in Godot within the two week development pattern because a more versatile engine was needed to achieve the visuals I had in mind.

The visuals in question were very focused on trying to convey a sense of character and world. With the small amount of time I had, I went with a very limited color scheme and simplistic contour style in order to create as many assets as possible and fill the world with a density of detail that would immerse the player and keep them engaged.

Independent Project

Rotscoped Dancer

This project is a rotoscoped version of some obscure internet meme that had been around for years that I particularly loved. I found the combination of Tame Impala and this biker guys movements were more than the sum of their parts and I wanted to try to capture that by distilling the visuals down the most important elements. To this end I chose to rotoscope the dancer with a very expressive painterly style that captured the energy of the movement and also matched the very low res video. After completing that I went back and traced in the dog and the bike in the background too as I felt they were vital elements of the unique identity of the work.
